uleå University of Technology

In the far north, way beyond the African origin jungles; In the cold dark, where sometimes even the day lacks light; There lies a University, on the edge of civilisation, pushing the frontier.

Stylish intros aside, LTU is comprised of five campuses: Kiruna, Skellefteå, Filipstad, Piteå and of course Luelå. In Kiruna the main focus of education is Space engineering and the campus is located close to Esrange. In Skellefteå the education focuses on game development and design. Mining and Metallurgy is the focus for the campus in Filipstad while music is what Piteå focuses on. The main campus in Luleå provides a plethora of educational opportunities.

History

In times long past, way, way back in 1971, the first stone to what would become Scandinavia's nothernmost university. Back in these days it was called "Högskoleenheten i Luleå", university status was not granted until 1997. A this time the named changed into what can still be recognised today, "Luleå University of Technology".

Organisation

Vice-Chancellor

The highest representative of the university is the vice-chancellor; appointed by the government they make sure that the university works like a well-oiled machine.

The current chancellor is called Johan Sterte, a researcher turned-chancellor, was instated in 2009 and has previously been responsible for creating the chemistry education at LTU.

Previous vice-chancellors include

  1. Lars Nordström
  2. Torbjörn Hedberg
  3. Ingegerd Palmér
  4. Pia Sandvik Wiklund
  5. Johan Sterte

Head of Administration

Is the second-in-command at the ship that is the university. He shoulders some of the responsibilities that are appointed to the vice-chancellor, such as acting as Head Librarian.

The current Head is Staffan Sarbäck, so if you have any problems with your work you can start by contacting him. His contact information can be found here.

University Board

The board is the most authoritative decision-making instance at the university and is composed of a number of people. Some are appointed by the government, some are elected by the teachers at the university, some are employed for power vested in them. In total there are fifteen people with voting powers, but more have the capacity to attend and speak.

The current chairperson is Eva Nordmark. Together with the rest of the board she presides over the creation of operational budgets and annual reports, among other things.

Professional Services

The professional services are a university-wide entity that ensures that the departments are running well. To achieve this they have distributed their activities to the departments and also provide overarching organisation at a central location.

The current head of the professional services is Staffan Sarbäck, which also happens to be the HEad of Administration. So if you have any problems, he should be sure to know. His contact information can be found here.

Faculty Boards

There are two faculty boards that help govern the education and organisational decisions needed for university operation. These are The Board of the Faculty of Science and Technology and The Board of the Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences; The first handles technical decision and the latter covers the humanity side.

Purpose

The purpose of LTU is to be a leading technical university, enabling attractive and sustainable solutions for modern problems. In order to achieve this goal there are a number of aspects that the university cares for. These are research, education and collaboration and are discussed below.

Research

LTU provides research opportunities for a wide variety of subjects but there are a few subjects in which the university specialises. These are

  • Attractive civil environments
  • Effective innovation and organisation
  • Future mining
  • Renewable energy
  • Innovative art and science
  • Sustainable transportation systems
  • Intelligent industrial processes
  • Enabling ICT
  • Smart machines and materials

These research subjects form the basis on which the operation of LTU rests. For more information about a specific subject, the interested reader is advised to peruse ltu.se.

Ball bearings

One area where LTU is a centre of excellence is the area of ball bearing research. Recently a university technology centre, UTC, associated with SKF was established at LTU. Within this centre both master students and PhD. are invited to develop the cutting edge of condition monitoring. Just to mention one project developed there; A high-frequency sensor that mimics the processing performed by the human brain to perform analysis.

This UTC is a global collaboration with the company SKF, but similar joint efforts are also conducted locally within the Luleå area. More can be read below.

Education

LTU is a technological university and therefore provides mainly technical education. In Sweden a combined program corresponding to a bachelor and master's degree is the main form of education but the master and bachelor exists as separate entities as well. The areas which one can pursue at LTU are listed below.

  • Engineering Physics and Engineering Electronics
  • Computer Science and Engineering
  • Space Engineering
  • Fire Engineering
  • Architectural Engineering
For a complete list an interested reader is kindly advised to take a look at the university homepage, ltu.se.

Local collaborations

LTU entertains good relationships with many local industry leaders. For example, LKAB and SSAB are to big partners that contribute to the research effort. In return LTU provides the people and the skills required to develop the cutting-edge. Many master's theses are written while working for one of these two companies, mainly within the material sciences, but there are many disciplines that are combined. Image analysis to automatically classify granules, thus improving pellet quality just to mention one.

Once again the interested reader is invited to read more on the respective web pages of the university departments.
